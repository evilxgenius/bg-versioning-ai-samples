J. B. Stephens Elementary School welcomed 377 students in the 2023-24 school year, according to the Texas Education Agency.

That's 9.8% lower than the previous school year, when the school enrolled 418 students.

Of all the students welcomed in the 2023-24 school year, 51.2% of them were boys, and 48.8% were girls.

Data also showed that most of the students were white, making up 69.5% of the J. B. Stephens Elementary School's total enrollment.

The school is located in Bangs ISD with a central office in Bangs.

In total, there were 5,531,236 students enrolled in Texas in the 2023-24 school year.

Underfunding is a frequently cited challenge facing Texas' school districts. According to a 2024 report from the Texas Education Agency, per-pupil funding has not increased since 2019, despite inflation rates rising by more than 20% since then.

“As a result, many districts in our very own Central Texas region are being forced to cut back on essential programs, services, consider school closures, and adopt deficit budgets just to provide students with the education that they deserve,” Hutto ISD Trustee James Matlock stated in an interview.
